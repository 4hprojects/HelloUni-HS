Graph 2 – Use Prim’s Algorithm

Consider another connected, undirected weighted graph with vertices {P, Q, R, S} and the following edges: 

P - Q  (weight 4)
P - R  (weight 2)
Q - R  (weight 1)
Q - S  (weight 5)
R - S  (weight 3)

Start Prim’s algorithm from vertex P.

How to solve using Prim’s Algorithm:
1. Start at the given vertex (here, P).
2. At each step, look at all edges connecting the tree to vertices not yet in the tree.
3. Choose the edge with the smallest weight that connects to a new vertex.
4. Add that edge and vertex to the tree.
5. Repeat until all vertices are included.

Tip: Always pick the lowest-weight edge that expands your tree, even if it’s not from the most recently added vertex.
